# Book 9: The Emotional Side of Periods
## Mood, Hormones, and Self‑Compassion

### Introduction
Periods affect more than the body — they influence emotions, thoughts, and relationships. This book explores the emotional side of menstruation, helping women understand their feelings and embrace self‑compassion.

---

### Chapter 1: Hormones and Mood
Hormones shift throughout the cycle, influencing emotions.  
- Estrogen boosts mood and energy.  
- Progesterone can cause calmness or fatigue.  
- Drops in hormones before menstruation may trigger PMS.  

---

### Chapter 2: Common Emotional Experiences
- Irritability or mood swings.  
- Sensitivity and heightened emotions.  
- Anxiety or sadness.  
- Creativity and reflection.  

---

### Chapter 3: Coping Strategies
- Journaling to track emotions.  
- Mindfulness and meditation.  
- Talking with friends or family.  
- Rest and self‑care rituals.  

---

### Chapter 4: Stories of Emotional Strength
- *Lina, 18:* “I cry more easily before my period, but I’ve learned it’s my body asking for rest.”  
- *Maria, 25:* “I use my luteal phase to write poetry. My emotions fuel my creativity.”  

---

### Conclusion
The emotional side of periods is not weakness — it’s part of the rhythm of womanhood. By understanding and embracing these feelings, women can find strength, compassion, and balance.
