# Book 7: Exercise and Periods
## Staying Active Safely

### Introduction
For many women, the idea of exercising during menstruation feels intimidating. Yet movement can be one of the most powerful tools for easing discomfort, boosting mood, and reclaiming confidence. This book explores how exercise interacts with the menstrual cycle, offering guidance, stories, and encouragement.

---

### Chapter 1: Why Movement Matters
Exercise increases blood flow, reduces cramps, and releases endorphins — your body’s natural painkillers. It also helps regulate hormones and improves sleep.  
✨ *Reflection:* Think of exercise as a gentle friend during your cycle, not an enemy.

---

### Chapter 2: Best Exercises for Each Phase
- **Menstrual Phase:** Gentle yoga, stretching, walking.  
- **Follicular Phase:** Cardio, strength training, new routines.  
- **Ovulation:** High‑energy workouts, dance, team sports.  
- **Luteal Phase:** Moderate exercise, Pilates, restorative yoga.  

---

### Chapter 3: Stories of Strength
- *Ama, 20:* “Running during my period felt impossible until I tried slow jogging. Now it’s my therapy.”  
- *Lina, 18:* “Yoga helps me breathe through cramps. It feels like medicine without pills.”  

---

### Chapter 4: Tips for Comfort
- Wear breathable clothing.  
- Stay hydrated.  
- Listen to your body — rest when needed.  

---

### Conclusion
Exercise during menstruation is not about pushing harder; it’s about moving with kindness. Your body is strong, and movement can be your ally.
