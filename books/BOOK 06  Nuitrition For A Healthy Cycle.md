# Book 6: Nutrition for a Healthy Cycle
## Foods That Help

### Introduction
Food is fuel, and during menstruation, the right nutrition can ease discomfort and support your body. This book explores how diet influences your cycle and offers practical tips for eating well.

---

### Chapter 1: Iron‑Rich Foods
Menstrual bleeding can lower iron levels. Replenish with:  
- Spinach and kale  
- Beans and lentils  
- Lean meats and fish  

✨ *Tip:* Pair iron‑rich foods with vitamin C (like oranges) to boost absorption.

---

### Chapter 2: Hydration
Water reduces bloating and fatigue. Herbal teas soothe cramps.  
- Chamomile tea calms nerves.  
- Peppermint tea eases digestion.  

---

### Chapter 3: Anti‑Inflammatory Choices
Ginger, turmeric, and omega‑3s calm inflammation and pain.  
✨ *Story:* *Aisha, 30, adds turmeric to her meals. “It’s my golden comfort,” she says.*

---

### Chapter 4: Foods to Limit
Too much caffeine or processed sugar can worsen cramps and mood swings.  
✨ *Tip:* Swap soda for sparkling water with lemon.

---

### Chapter 5: Cycle‑Friendly Meal Plan
- **Menstrual Phase:** Warm soups, iron‑rich stews.  
- **Follicular Phase:** Fresh salads, protein for energy.  
- **Ovulation:** Colorful fruits, lean proteins.  
- **Luteal Phase:** Complex carbs, herbal teas, comfort foods.  

---

### Conclusion
Eating with awareness during your cycle is a form of self‑love. Nourish your body, and it will thank you with energy, balance, and resilience.
